# Skill Gap Analysis

Use this guide to compare the user's qualifications against a specific job posting and provide actionable recommendations.

## Process

### 1. Extract Required Skills

From the job description, categorize requirements into:
- **Must-have**: Explicitly listed as required qualifications
- **Preferred**: Listed as preferred, nice-to-have, or "bonus"
- **Implied**: Not listed but clearly needed based on role responsibilities

### 2. Map Against User's Profile

Cross-reference each requirement against:
- `references/user_profile.md` for key competencies
- The baseline resume for detailed experience
- LinkedIn profile for additional context

Classify each skill as:
- **Strong match**: Direct, demonstrable experience
- **Partial match**: Related experience that's transferable
- **Gap**: No direct or adjacent experience

### 3. Assess Gap Severity

Not all gaps are equal. Evaluate each gap by:

- **Criticality**: Is this a must-have or a nice-to-have?
- **Learnability**: Can it be acquired quickly (tool proficiency) or slowly (domain expertise)?
- **Adjacency**: Does the user have closely related experience that reduces the gap?
- **Market context**: Is this a common gap that employers often overlook, or a true dealbreaker?

### 4. Provide Recommendations

For each gap:

**If the gap is addressable in the application:**
- Suggest how to frame transferable skills to cover the gap
- Identify specific resume bullets that can be reworded to bridge the gap
- Recommend cover letter language that acknowledges growth areas with confidence

**If the gap requires upskilling:**
- Suggest specific courses, certifications, or resources
- Prioritize free or low-cost options (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, official docs)
- Estimate effort level (weekend project vs. multi-month commitment)
- Recommend whether to upskill before or after applying

**If the gap is a dealbreaker:**
- Be honest about whether the role is realistic
- Suggest alternative roles at the same company that may be a better fit
- Recommend a stepping-stone path if the user is committed to this direction

## Output Format

Present the analysis as a table for quick scanning:

| Requirement | User's Match | Gap Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Skill] | Strong/Partial/Gap | Critical/Moderate/Low | [Action] |
