# Networking Support

Use this guide to help the user build connections at target companies and expand their professional network strategically.

## LinkedIn Connection Requests

### To Recruiters
- Keep under 300 characters (LinkedIn's limit for connection notes)
- Mention the specific role or team you're interested in
- Reference something specific about the company (not generic flattery)
- Example: "Hi [Name], I'm a TPM with 10+ years in [relevant domain] and I'm interested in the [Role] on your team. I'd love to connect and learn more about the opportunity."

### To Current Employees
- Find people in the target team or adjacent teams
- Reference shared connections, alma maters, or professional communities if applicable
- Ask for a brief informational conversation, not a referral upfront
- Example: "Hi [Name], I'm exploring TPM opportunities at [Company] and noticed your work on [specific project/team]. Would you be open to a brief chat about your experience there?"

### To Hiring Managers
- Only reach out if you've already applied or are about to
- Lead with a concise value proposition, not a request
- Reference the specific role by title
- Example: "Hi [Name], I recently applied for the [Role] on your team. My background in [key skill] at [Company] aligns closely with what you're building. I'd welcome the chance to discuss how I can contribute."

## Cold Outreach Emails

Structure for emails to hiring managers or team leads:

1. **Subject line**: Brief and specific — "[Role Title] — [Your Key Differentiator]"
2. **Opening**: Who you are and why you're reaching out (1-2 sentences)
3. **Value pitch**: 2-3 bullet points connecting your experience to their team's needs
4. **Ask**: A specific, low-commitment request (15-minute call, not "any time that works")
5. **Close**: Professional sign-off with LinkedIn profile link

Keep the total email under 150 words. Hiring managers skim.

## Networking Strategy

### Before Applying
- Identify 2-3 people at the target company to connect with
- Prioritize: team members > adjacent teams > recruiters
- Have informational conversations to learn about team culture, priorities, and pain points
- Use insights to strengthen your application materials

### After Applying
- Follow up with recruiter connections after 1 week if no response
- Do not follow up more than twice
- If you have an internal referral, ask them to submit it within 48 hours of your application

### Ongoing
- Engage with target company content on LinkedIn (thoughtful comments, not just likes)
- Share relevant industry content that positions you as knowledgeable in the space
- Attend industry meetups, webinars, or conferences where target company employees present
- Check `references/list_of_target_companies.md` for the full list of priority companies
