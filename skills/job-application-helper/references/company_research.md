# Company Research

Use this guide when researching a target company for cover letters, interview preparation, or application strategy.

## When to Research

- **Always** before writing a cover letter — never submit a cover letter without company-specific content
- When the user asks about a specific company
- When analyzing whether a role is a good fit

## What to Search For

**Always use web search** to find the following:

1. **Recent news and developments**
   - Press releases from the last 6 months
   - Product launches, partnerships, or acquisitions
   - Funding rounds, IPO activity, or revenue milestones
   - Leadership changes or strategic pivots

2. **Company culture indicators**
   - Glassdoor or Blind reviews (note trends, not individual reviews)
   - Engineering blog posts or tech talks
   - Open source contributions or community involvement
   - Remote/hybrid work policies

3. **Mission and values**
   - Official mission statement from company website
   - CEO or founder statements on company direction
   - DEI initiatives or sustainability commitments
   - How values show up in product decisions

4. **Industry position**
   - Key competitors and differentiators
   - Market share or growth trajectory
   - Industry awards or recognition
   - Analyst reports or media coverage

## How to Use Research

- **Cover letters**: Reference 1-2 specific recent developments that connect to the user's experience or career goals
- **Interview prep**: Prepare informed questions that demonstrate genuine interest and knowledge
- **Fit analysis**: Flag potential concerns (recent layoffs, leadership instability, pivoting away from user's domain)

## Tips

- Prioritize information from the last 6 months for maximum relevance
- Cross-reference multiple sources — don't rely on a single article
- Focus on developments relevant to the specific role, not just the company overall
- Note the company's tech stack if mentioned in engineering blogs or job postings
