# Google Hiring Assessment — Scoring Guide

## How Google Scores the GHA

Based on community reports, recruiter insights, and candidate experiences:

- **Automated scoring**: Aggregate patterns compared against benchmarks from successful past applicants
- **Consistency weighting**: Paired questions (same concept, different wording) are checked for alignment — contradictions reduce your score significantly
- **Extreme response preference**: Successful candidates report using Strongly Agree / Strongly Disagree for 90-95% of answers
- **No single question eliminates you**: Scoring is holistic across all categories
- **Some questions are experimental**: Included for future assessment development, not scored (you won't know which)

## Red Flag Patterns

Flag these patterns across a user's full set of answers:

1. **Ethics flexibility**: Any answers suggesting rules can be bent for convenience
2. **Lone-wolf tendency**: Pattern of preferring independent work, avoiding team input, or centralizing decisions
3. **Passivity**: Waiting for others to act, avoiding initiative, deferring unnecessarily
4. **Rigidity**: Unwillingness to change course, strong attachment to being right
5. **Blame-shifting**: Deflecting responsibility for mistakes
6. **Neutrality overuse**: More than 10% of answers as Neutral/middle suggests indecisiveness
7. **Inconsistency**: Any paired question mismatch
8. **Negation misread**: Agreeing with negatively-worded statements that should be disagreed with (e.g., Q84, Q86, Q87)
